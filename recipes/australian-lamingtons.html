Australian Lamingtons

There is uncertainty as to the origin of Lamingtons. Named after Lord and Lady Lamington of Queensland, this special desert is generally known to have originated from Australia. However, some say it was created in New Zealand. Regardless of its origin, this square spongy cake coated in dark chocolate icing and tossed about in tossed about in dessicated coconut has now been adopted the world over, particularly in Croatia where it is known as Cupavci.

Ingredients

The ingredients for lamingtons are common ingredients used in a lot of other cakes.

Ingredients for the butter Sponge

  • 230 g of unsalted butter at room temperature
  • 250 g of granulated sugar
  • 3 large eggs at room temperature
  • 250 g of all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 120 ml of whole milk
  • 3 teaspoons of baking powder
  • half a teaspoon of fine salt

Ingredients for chocolate icing and Coconut

  • 50 g of cocoa powder
  • 250 g of powdered sugar
  • 60 g of unsalted butter, melted
  • 180 ml of boiling water
  • 200 g of dessicated coconut

Steps

Preparing the Sponge Cake and the glaze

  1. Preheat the oven to 180 degree celcius.
  2. Mix the butter and sugar together until pale and creamy.
  3. Now, add the eggs, one at a time, while continuing to mix, until combined.
  4. In a separate bowl, sift and mix the flour and baking powder, then add the salt.
  5. Add the dry ingredients to the batter and mix until combined. Then add the milk and mix again until combined.
  6. Grease the base and sides of the barking pan with butter, then line the pan with parchment paper.
  7. Transfer the batter into the baking pan and place the pan into the preheated oven. Bake for 40-45 mins. Use a skewer to test for preparedness: once a skewer comes out clean, then the cake is baked. Remove from the oven and let cool.
  8. Once cool, trim the edges with a serrated break knife, then cut the sponge into squares. Freeze for 30-40 mins until quite cold but not frozen through.
  9. Prepare the glaze:Sift the powdered sugar and cocoa powder into a bowl and add in the butter and boiling water. Whisk the mixture until smooth and combined; should be runny.

Glazing

  1. Remove the cubes of sponge cake from the freezer for glazing. Spread the dessicated coconut in a shallow bowl. Also pour the chocolate glaze in a shallow bowl placed on boiling wate to avoid thickening and place a wire rack on a a baking tray nearby.
  2. Use two forks to pick each cube, then dip it in the glaze to coat all sides. Lift out of the glaze and and let the excess drip off on the wire rack.
  3. Once the excess has dripped off, dip the cube in the bowl of dessicated coconut, all sides. Once covered on all sides, roll again in the cocunut with your fingers to ensure perfect coating to avoid stickiness. Repeat the process until all the cubes are coated. Leave on a separate rack for about an hour to set.
